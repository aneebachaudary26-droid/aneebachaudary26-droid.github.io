About

I develop fluorine‑free omniphobic coatings for textiles by marrying Janus nanoparticles with crosslinkable acrylic/silane binders. My work targets durable repellency via hierarchical roughness, stratification, and re‑entrant geometries while maintaining breathability and sustainability.

Keywords: surface engineering, wetting, dynamic covalent chemistry, coatings durability, contact‑angle goniometry, AFM/SEM, confocal, thermal curing.

Research Focus

PFAS‑free Omniphobic Textiles

PS‑co‑PVA/MPTS and PS‑PAA@SO3⁻/MPTS Janus nanoparticles integrated with BA‑based binders and thermal crosslinkers to create re‑entrant topographies and low liquid adhesion.

Stratification & Re‑entrant Design

Controlling particle size ratios and curing to drive top‑layer enrichment, double re‑entrant features, and robust CA/SA performance against oils.

DCC & Self‑Assembly

Imine/hydrazone macrocycles and cages to probe structure–function relationships in host–guest systems relevant to barrier coatings.

Selected Projects

PFAS‑free Fabric Repellency

Formulating BA binders (DVB/NMA) with JNPs; optimizing cure and particle size distribution (100–500 nm) for oil/water repellency and wash durability.

Role: lead synthesis, coating, CA/SA testing, AFM roughness analysis, ANOVA stats.

Nanocellulose/Chitosan Aerogels

Thermal management and oil‑sorbent aerogels; structure–property tuning via ice templating and silica coatings.

Surface Silanization

MPTS/DCDMS vapor treatments to engineer Si–O–Si networks and enhance binder adhesion and weatherability.
